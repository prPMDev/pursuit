# My Profile — Example (Alex, Senior PM)

This is an example of a filled-in profile. Yours should be this specific.

---

## Professional Identity

**Who are you in one sentence?**
I'm a product manager who builds integrations, APIs, and partner ecosystems at B2B SaaS companies.

**Your brand / what people know you for:**
The "glue person" — I connect systems, teams, and companies. Former colleagues say I'm the one who figured out the partner API strategy and made eng and BD actually work together.

**Years of experience:** 8 years in product management

---

## Confidence Zones

**What you're great at and know it:**
API strategy, partner/integration ecosystem design, cross-functional alignment (eng + BD + partnerships), technical product specs, developer experience.

**What you're good at but still building:**
Data-driven product decisions (can do it, want deeper analytics chops), stakeholder management at VP+ level.

**What you want to learn / grow into:**
Platform strategy at a larger scale (100+ integrations), people management (haven't managed PMs yet).

---

## Level & Leveling

**Current level:** Senior PM

**Target level:** Senior PM or Staff/Principal PM

**Leveling context:**
Staff PM at a 200-person B2B SaaS ≈ Senior PM at a FAANG. I'm open to either title as long as the scope includes strategy + execution, not just execution. At a startup (sub-100), "Head of Product" with a team of 1 is fine — it's about scope, not title.

---

## Compensation

**Target range:** $185-220k base

**Flexibility:**
Would take $160k at a pre-Series B with meaningful equity (0.1%+ with clear vesting). Not flexible below $160k — mortgage and family.

---

## Location & Work Style

**Location:** NYC metro area

**Remote preference:** Remote-first or hybrid (2 days/week max). Full onsite = pass.

**Relocation:** Not relocating. Would consider SF for a truly exceptional opportunity, but it's a high bar.

---

## Non-Negotiables

- Remote or NYC hybrid only — no full onsite, no relocation required
- Must include strategy scope (not pure execution/ticket-taking)
- Series A+ only — no pre-revenue, no consulting shops
- No defense/weapons companies

---

## Risk Appetite

**Where I play safe:**
At big companies (1000+), I want direct domain match — integrations/API/platform. I don't want to fight the leveling battle AND learn a completely new product area at the same time.

**Where I'll stretch:**
At startups, open to a title bump (Staff PM) even if scope is partly new. Also fine switching industries (fintech → healthtech → commerce) as long as the structural work is the same (integrations, platform, partner ecosystem).

**How to calibrate risk for me:**
1 stretch factor = great. New domain OR level up, not both at a big company. At a startup, I'll accept 2 factors if equity is meaningful.

---

## Personal Red Flags

- Companies with 3+ PMs on the same product (too many cooks, scope gets carved up)
- "PM" that actually means project manager (look for program/project management signals)
- Job posts listing 15+ requirements (unrealistic expectations, likely a committee-designed role)
- Companies that just did layoffs in product — means they don't value the function
- "Fast-paced environment" in every paragraph (usually means no process, constant firefighting)

---

## Culture & Personality Fit

**Work pace:**
I like structured chaos — clear goals but room to figure out the "how." Not waterfall, not pure YOLO.

**Team size preference:**
Small-to-mid product teams (2-5 engineers per squad). I want to know the engineers by name.

**What energizes me:**
Solving the "how do we connect these two systems/teams/companies" puzzle. Shipping integrations that unlock new use cases. Teaching non-technical stakeholders how APIs work.

**What drains me:**
Pure stakeholder management with no building. Roadmap theater. Being 3 layers removed from customers.
